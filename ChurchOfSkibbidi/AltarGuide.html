Common offerings

  • Candles Candles symbolize divine light and were historically used in rituals to honor deities or guide prayers.(real or fake)
  • Flowers (real or fake) Flowers represent beauty and life, often used in ancient ceremonies as offerings to gods to signify purity and growth.(real or fake)
  • A depiction of the God (Picture, statue, sculpture, etc.) Depictions of gods help worshippers connect with them, historically seen in statues or paintings to focus prayers and offerings.
  • Symbolism of what your god rules over Symbolic items, like a harvest tool for an agricultural deity, have historically been offerings to invoke blessings over specific domains.
  • Some of your Art of them or symbols associated with them Art honors deities by showcasing personal devotion, with historical use in frescoes, carvings, or handmade crafts dedicated to gods.
  • Incense Incense has been used for millennia in ceremonies to purify spaces and carry prayers through its sacred smoke.
  • Monetary offerings Money symbolizes gratitude and was historically given to support temples, shrines, and religious communities.
  • Soaps, Jewelry, and Perfumes These luxurious offerings historically symbolized respect and appreciation, often gifted to gods or sacred figures.
  • Water, Wine, and Alcohols Liquids have been key offerings in libations, symbolizing life, abundance, and gratitude to the divine throughout history.
